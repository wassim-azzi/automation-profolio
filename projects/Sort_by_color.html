Automatic Color Sorting and Storage System

Industrial Automation Project

Industrial automation project developed using Siemens STEP 7 (LAD) and Factory I/O

Project Overview

The Automatic Color Sorting and Storage System is an industrial automation project developed using Siemens STEP 7 (LAD) and Factory I/O. The system automatically identifies the color of workpieces traveling on a conveyor belt, sorts them accordingly, and stores them in designated locations using PLC-based control logic.

Engineering Problem

Manual sorting and storage of products are repetitive, time-consuming, and susceptible to human errors. This project automates the entire process to improve efficiency, accuracy, and production consistency.

Components Used

Air Preparation Unit (FRL)

Filters, regulates, and lubricates the compressed air.

Double-Acting Cylinders

Two pneumatic cylinders for clamping and drilling movements.

5/2 Directional Valves

Used to control the extension and retraction of each cylinder.

3/2 Mechanically Actuated Valves

Detect the workpiece and the end positions of Cylinders A and B.

3/2 Push Button

Starts the cycle.

2/n Way Valve

Pneumatic logic AND element that ensures required conditions are satisfied before the next operation.

ON-Delay Timer

Maintains the drilling operation for 3 seconds.

Off-Delay Timer

Maintains the output signal for 1 second.

Drill Motor

Performs drilling.

Operating Sequence

  1. The operator places the workpiece and presses the Start push button.
  2. The presence of the workpiece is detected, and Cylinder A extends to clamp it.
  3. Cylinder B moves downward, bringing the drilling tool into contact with the workpiece.
  4. The drilling motor starts and drills for 3 seconds.
  5. After the delay, the motor stops and Cylinder B retracts to its initial position.
  6. Cylinder A retracts, releasing the drilled workpiece.
  7. The station returns to its initial state, ready for the next cycle.

GRAFCET

The GRAFCET represents the sequential control of the drilling station. Each transition corresponds to a sensor signal or the completion of a previous operation, ensuring that the drilling cycle proceeds safely and in the correct order.

Pneumatic Circuit

The pneumatic circuit consists of compressed-air preparation components, directional control valves, sensors, and two double-acting cylinders. Cylinder A performs the clamping operation, while Cylinder B controls the vertical movement of the drilling tool. Directional valves and sensors coordinate the sequence, ensuring each actuator moves only after the previous operation has been completed.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge

During testing, Cylinder B restarted unexpectedly after returning to its initial position.

Solution

The control sequence was revised to eliminate the repeated trigger, ensuring that each movement is executed only once per cycle.

Lessons Learned

  • Improved understanding of industrial pneumatic automation.
  • Developed skills in pneumatic circuit design.
  • Applied the GRAFCET methodology to sequence operations.
  • Integrated timers into the drilling cycle.
  • Troubleshot and corrected an unexpected cylinder restart during testing.
  • Validated the complete operating sequence through simulation.
